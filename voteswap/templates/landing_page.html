How does it work?

When you join voteswap you will be matched with a voter in your Facebook friends network. Your match will be someone your know personally or a friend of a friend.

You're limited to swapping with someone in your network because we think this will keep everyone honest. You can trust your friends to follow through on their pledge to swap and save the presidency.

You can propose vote swaps with several friends. We automatically rank your friends by the impact. If you're in a safe state, friends in important swing states are shown first. If you're in a swing state, then friends in very safe states are shown first.

After proposing swaps and talking to several friends, you can accept one swap. On election day we'll send you both an email to remind you who you're swapping with.

After the election

After the election, while the surprisingly high third-party turnout is still on everyone's minds, we can start a dialogue on changing our election process to use ranked choice voting, aka instant runoff elections. This system of voting lets you write down your candidates in order, so if your first choice doesn't win, your vote goes to your second choice, and so on. This allows votes for third parties (or fourth, or fifth, or ...) to not be wasted and let the least favored candidate win.

Ranked choice voting is used around the world: cities and states in the United States, congressional elections in Australia, and for various elections in India, New Zealand, Ireland, and the United Kingdom.

Ranked choice voting is not without its flaws, but they can be addressed by using the Condorcet method to count votes. Condorcet voting prevents a few counter-intuitive outcomes of ranked choice voting, and should be our preferred method.
